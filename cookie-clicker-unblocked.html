What is Cookie Clicker?

Cookie Clicker was created by Julien "Orteil" Thiennot and released in 2013. It is widely credited as the game that invented the idle clicker genre — a style of game that has since spawned thousands of imitators. The premise is absurdly simple: click a giant cookie to produce cookies, then spend those cookies on buildings that produce more cookies automatically.

But Cookie Clicker is far deeper than it appears. The upgrade tree has hundreds of items. The Grandmapocalypse storyline adds a dark, surreal narrative layer. The Ascension prestige system resets your progress in exchange for permanent multipliers, creating a satisfying loop of growth and reset. Players have spent thousands of hours in Cookie Clicker — and it's all free in the browser.

How to Play Cookie Clicker

  • Click the big cookie on the left side of the screen to produce cookies.
  • Buy buildings on the right side — Cursors, Grandmas, Farms, Mines, Factories, and more. Each building produces cookies per second automatically.
  • Buy upgrades in the top-left panel when they appear. Upgrades multiply your production and unlock new mechanics.
  • Click Golden Cookies when they appear on screen — they give massive temporary boosts like "Frenzy" (7x production for 77 seconds) or "Lucky" (instant cookie windfall).
  • Ascend once you have enough Heavenly Chips to make it worthwhile — this resets your run but gives permanent multipliers that make your next run far faster.

Cookie Clicker Strategy Guide

1. Always buy upgrades immediately

Upgrades are almost always more efficient than buying more buildings. When an upgrade appears in the store, buy it as soon as you can afford it. Many upgrades double or triple the output of specific buildings — delaying them costs you enormous production over time.

2. Never miss a Golden Cookie

Golden Cookies appear randomly on screen and disappear after a few seconds. Clicking them gives you powerful temporary boosts. The "Frenzy" buff (7x production) combined with a "Click Frenzy" buff (777x click power) is the most powerful combination in the game — save your manual clicking for these moments.

3. The Grandmapocalypse is worth triggering

Many players avoid the Grandmapocalypse because it seems scary. Don't. Triggering it unlocks Wrinklers — creatures that appear to eat your cookies but actually multiply them when you pop them. Wrinklers are one of the most efficient passive income sources in the game.

4. Time your first Ascension carefully

Don't Ascend too early. Your first Ascension should be when you have at least 365 Heavenly Chips (for the "Lucky Day" upgrade chain). Ascending with fewer chips wastes the reset. Check a Cookie Clicker Ascension guide for the optimal first-run upgrades to buy with your chips.

5. Use the Grimoire spell "Force the Hand of Fate"

Once you unlock the Wizard Tower and the Grimoire, the "Force the Hand of Fate" spell summons a Golden Cookie instantly. Cast it during a Frenzy buff to stack multipliers for astronomical cookie gains.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Cookie Clicker free to play?

Yes! Cookie Clicker is completely free to play in the browser at orteil.dashnet.org. The Steam version costs $4.99 and includes extra features, but the web version is the full original game at no cost.

What is Ascension in Cookie Clicker?

Ascension is a prestige mechanic where you reset your game in exchange for Heavenly Chips, which give permanent multipliers to your cookie production. It's the key to reaching truly astronomical cookie counts in later runs.

What should I buy first in Cookie Clicker?

Buy Cursors and Grandmas first to establish a base income. Then prioritize Farms and Mines. Always buy upgrades as soon as they appear — they often double or triple your production and are almost always more efficient than buying more buildings.

Does Cookie Clicker save progress?

Yes. Cookie Clicker saves your progress automatically in your browser's local storage. As long as you don't clear your browser data, your save will be there when you return. You can also export your save to a text string to back it up.
