What is Minecraft?

Minecraft is the best-selling video game of all time, with over 300 million copies sold across all platforms. Created by Markus "Notch" Persson and released in 2011 by Mojang, Minecraft is a sandbox game set in an infinite procedurally generated world made entirely of blocks. Players can mine resources, craft tools, build structures, and survive against monsters — or simply explore and create in Creative mode with unlimited blocks.

The browser version available here is Minecraft Classic — the original 2009 version of the game, released for free by Mojang to celebrate Minecraft's 10th anniversary. It's a pure building experience with 32 block types and supports up to 32 players in the same world. While it lacks the survival mechanics and modern features of the full game, it's a perfect way to experience the original Minecraft in your browser with no download required.

How to Play Minecraft Classic

  • Move: WASD keys to walk, Space to jump, Shift to sneak.
  • Look around: Move your mouse to look. Click inside the game window to lock your cursor.
  • Break blocks: Left-click and hold on a block to break it.
  • Place blocks: Right-click to place the selected block.
  • Select blocks: Press E to open your inventory and select which block to place. Use the number keys 1–9 to switch between hotbar slots.
  • Fly: In Classic mode, press Space twice to toggle flying. Use Space to go up and Shift to go down while flying.
  • Multiplayer: Share the URL with friends to build together in the same world.

Minecraft Survival Tips (Full Game)

1. Your first priority is wood

The moment you spawn, punch trees. You need wood to craft a crafting table, which unlocks the full crafting grid. From wood you can make wooden tools, which let you mine stone faster. From stone you can make stone tools, which are significantly more durable. This progression — wood → stone → iron → diamond — is the core loop of Minecraft's early game.

2. Build shelter before your first night

You have about 10 minutes of in-game daylight before monsters spawn. Use that time to gather wood, craft basic tools, and build a small shelter. It doesn't need to be pretty — a 5×5 dirt box with a door is enough. The first night is the most dangerous moment in any Minecraft run.

3. Light up your base

Monsters spawn in darkness. Place torches everywhere inside and around your base to prevent spawning. Torches are cheap — just a stick and a piece of coal or charcoal. Charcoal is made by smelting wood logs in a furnace, so you don't even need to find coal to get started.

4. Never dig straight down

This is the most important rule in Minecraft. Digging straight down can drop you into a cave, a lava pool, or a ravine with no warning. Always dig at an angle or use a staircase pattern when mining downward.

5. Find iron as fast as possible

Iron tools and armor are a massive upgrade over stone. Iron is found underground, typically between Y-levels 0 and 63. Once you have iron, you can make a bucket (to move water and lava), a shield (to block attacks), and full armor that makes you much harder to kill.

Frequently Asked Questions

What version of Minecraft can I play unblocked?

The browser version is Minecraft Classic (2009), the original version of the game. It's free, requires no download, and lets you place and break blocks in a 32-player multiplayer world. For the full modern Minecraft experience with survival, crafting, and all modern features, you need the paid Java or Bedrock edition.

How do I survive my first night in Minecraft?

Punch trees to get wood, craft a crafting table, make wooden tools, mine stone, and build a small shelter before dark. The first night is the most dangerous — monsters spawn in the dark and will attack you. Even a dirt hut is enough to survive. Light the inside with torches to prevent spawning.

What is the difference between Survival and Creative mode?

In Survival mode, you gather resources, manage hunger, and fight monsters. In Creative mode, you have unlimited blocks, can fly, and take no damage. Creative is perfect for building projects; Survival is the core gameplay experience that most players consider the "real" Minecraft.

Can I play Minecraft with friends for free?

Yes! Minecraft Classic supports up to 32 players in the same world. Just share the URL of your Classic session with friends and they can join. For the full multiplayer experience with survival mechanics, you'd need the paid version and a server.
